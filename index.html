My Story

From logistics founder to data analyst

For years I owned and operated a logistics business in Hamburg — optimising routes, forecasting demand, and segmenting customers using whatever data tools I had. Every decision had a real cost. That pressure taught me something most analysts learn in theory: data only matters when it changes a decision.

Transitioning into analytics wasn't a career change — it was adding Python, SQL, and Tableau to a toolkit I'd already been building for years. I bring both the technical depth and the business context to turn insights into actions.

Business-first mindset: I start with "What problem are we solving?" — not "What can the data show?"
Operational fluency: Years running logistics operations means I understand process optimisation from the ground up
Multilingual reach: Fluent in English, German, Italian, Spanish, and French — cultural awareness for global data challenges
Clear communication: I translate technical findings into recommendations non-technical stakeholders can act on
