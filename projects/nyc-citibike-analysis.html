Project Overview

Core Question

How can we predict demand fluctuations and optimise bike distribution across 1,500+ stations to maximise availability during peak hours and seasons?

Business Value

An interactive operational dashboard with demand forecasting, station performance analytics, and seasonal scaling recommendations — reducing costs while improving customer satisfaction.

Seasonal Strategy Insights

  • Clear correlation: 0.82 temperature-usage correlation confirms weather as the primary demand driver — more reliable than any other variable tested
  • Peak season: May–Oct accounts for 70% of annual ridership — concentrated resource allocation in these months maximises ROI
  • Winter opportunity: Nov–Apr shows 40–50% lower demand — the ideal window for maintenance, upgrades, and fleet reduction
  • Revenue uplift: Dynamic pricing during peak summer months projected to increase annual revenue by 20–25%

Operational Insights & Strategic Impact

This analysis translates usage patterns into concrete operational and financial decisions. Here is what the data means for each team within the organisation.

Critical Findings

Weather-Driven Demand

The 0.82 temperature-usage correlation is strong enough to build a reliable forecast model. Summer peaks at 140K daily trips vs winter lows of 45K — a 3× swing that creates predictable cost and staffing pressures if not planned for in advance.

Station Concentration Risk

The top 5 stations handle 3× the average trip volume, creating bottlenecks during peak hours. This concentration means a small number of stations disproportionately determine customer satisfaction ratings across the whole network.

Seasonal Cost Opportunity

November–April shows 40–50% lower demand. This is a structured opportunity to reduce fleet costs by up to 40%, redirect staff to maintenance, and upgrade infrastructure without service disruption.

Recommendations

Operations Management

Implement seasonal fleet scaling — reduce active bikes by 40% November through April, redirecting maintenance resources to station upgrades and bike servicing. This alone reduces operational costs by an estimated 30–40%.

Station Optimisation

Create priority rebalancing routes for the top 20 high-demand stations, with dedicated runs timed to the 7–9 AM and 5–7 PM commuter peaks. Stations falling below 20% capacity should trigger automatic rebalancing alerts.

Revenue Strategy

Introduce peak-season dynamic pricing (May–Oct) with 15–20% price premiums during weekends and holidays. This strategy is projected to increase annual revenue by 20–25% without meaningfully reducing ridership.

Customer Experience

Deploy predictive availability alerts in the mobile app during high-demand periods. When a station is forecast to hit low inventory within 30 minutes, users near that station receive a proactive notification directing them to the next nearest station.

Strategic Impact

Data-driven seasonal scaling reduces operational costs by 30–40%
Optimised resource allocation to high-demand stations improves satisfaction
Identified expansion opportunities in underserved residential areas
Dynamic pricing model increases potential annual revenue by 20–25%
Predictive maintenance scheduling in low-demand winter seasons
Rebalancing route optimisation reduces bike repositioning labour costs
