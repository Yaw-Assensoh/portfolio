Project Overview

Core Question

How can we segment customers based on purchasing behaviour and identify product associations to create targeted marketing campaigns that increase order frequency and basket size?

Business Value

A comprehensive customer segmentation model with personalised marketing recommendations, optimal ad timing, and product bundling strategies — translating purchase data into measurable revenue decisions.

Strategic Business Insights

  • Segmentation gap: The 2.3× AOV gap between Affluent Families and Students calls for fundamentally different marketing content, not just different budgets
  • Peak timing: Morning and evening spending peaks are the optimal windows for ad placement — outside these windows, ad spend efficiency drops significantly
  • National consistency: Consistent spending patterns across states mean nationwide campaigns are viable, with local adjustments only for high-spender concentration areas

Marketing Insights & Revenue Strategy

This analysis turns purchase data into a concrete marketing and revenue playbook. Each insight below maps directly to a team, a budget decision, and a projected outcome.

Critical Findings

Segmentation Clarity

35–37% of customers are 'High Spenders' (Affluent Families) with a 2.3× higher Average Order Value than the 'Students' segment. These two groups need completely different marketing messages — the same campaign cannot optimally serve both.

Peak Timing Precision

Spending surges by 40% during the 7–9 AM and 5–7 PM windows. Evening purchases show 15% higher basket diversity — customers browse more broadly in the evening and are more receptive to discovery-based recommendations.

Product Pair Intelligence

Market basket analysis identified 12 high-probability product pairs (pasta+sauce, cereal+milk) with 65%+ co-purchase rates. Bundling them at a small discount converts awareness into immediate action — customers were already planning to buy both.

Recommendations

Marketing Team

Run segment-specific campaigns: premium product promotions to Affluent Families (10 AM–12 PM), value deals to Students (7–9 PM). Projected outcome: 25% increase in conversion rates by matching message to customer type and timing window.

Product Management

Launch strategic product bundles based on the 12 identified pairs (pasta+sauce+cheese) with a 5% discount. Estimated impact: +15% Average Order Value and −18% cart abandonment. Customers were already buying these together — removing friction just confirms the sale faster.

Ad Operations

Allocate 40% of daily ad budget to the two peak windows (7–9 AM, 5–7 PM) and use remaining budget for retargeting high-intent afternoon browsers. This optimises Cost Per Acquisition by an estimated 30% without increasing total spend.

Customer Retention

Create 'Favorites Restock' automated reminders for High Spenders sent 3 days after their typical repurchase cycle. These customers are the most valuable segment — automating the repurchase prompt increases order frequency by an estimated 22%.

Strategic Impact

Targeted campaigns for Affluent Family segment — 25% projected conversion rate increase
Ad placement optimised to two peak spend windows — higher efficiency, same budget
Product bundling strategy built from real co-purchase data — not guesswork
Personalised recommendation logic based on 12 identified high-probability product pairs
Automated retention loop for High Spenders timed to their purchase cycle
National strategy validated by state-level consistency analysis across all regions
