Project Overview

Core Question

Why are some US cities becoming unaffordable while others remain stable — and can we predict where prices are heading?

Business Value

Provides homebuyers, policymakers, and real estate professionals with data-driven insights into affordability trends, market risk levels, and 3-year price forecasts across 5 major US metro areas.

Los Angeles — HIGH RISK New York — HIGH RISK Miami — MODERATE Chicago — LOW RISK Houston — LOW RISK

Affordability Index = Median Home Price ÷ (Annual Median Income × 5)
FindingInsight
The CrisisLA (index 2.22) and NY (1.58) have been unaffordable since before 2015. An LA household needs $200K+ annual income to afford a median home.
Warning SignMiami crossed the affordability threshold in 2022 — 120% home value growth since 2015, the fastest in the dataset.
Counter-NarrativeChicago and Houston stayed below 1.0 throughout the entire 10-year period.
COVID ImpactCOVID-19 (2020–2022) caused the sharpest simultaneous deterioration across all 5 cities. LA's index jumped 0.302 points in 2022 alone.
Root CauseMedian income grew $11K over 10 years while LA home values grew $400K. Income subsidies alone cannot fix a structural supply problem.

Phase 1 Insights

  • Threshold Breach: LA and NY were already above 1.0 in January 2015 — this crisis predates our dataset
  • COVID Acceleration: All 5 cities deteriorated simultaneously between 2020 and 2022
  • Miami Surprise: Grew 120% in home values, crossing the affordability threshold for the first time in 2022
  • Houston Model: Remained below 1.0 throughout — proving affordability is achievable in major metros

Phase 2 — SQL Market Intelligence

Market Risk Classification

PostgreSQL CASE WHEN query classifying all 5 cities into HIGH, MODERATE, and LOW risk categories based on affordability index thresholds.

LA is the only HIGH RISK market — avg index 1.745

Year-over-Year Change Analysis

LAG window function tracking annual changes in affordability index per city — isolating exactly when and where deterioration was fastest.

2021–2022 was the worst period — LA dropped 0.302 points in one year

Phase 2 Insights

  • Risk Confirmed: LA is the only HIGH RISK market — avg index 1.745. NY is MODERATE at 1.309
  • Rent Burden: NY rent hit 44% of income in 2024 — highest of all 5 cities
  • Growth Leaders: Miami led 10-year home value growth at 120.2%. NY was slowest at 59.4%
  • Threshold Crossing: Miami first crossed 1.0 in 2022. LA and NY were already above it in 2015
