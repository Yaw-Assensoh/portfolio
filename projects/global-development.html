Project Overview

Core Question

What are the key relationships between economic indicators, digital infrastructure, and human development outcomes across 200+ countries, and how can this inform targeted intervention strategies?

Business Value

An analytical framework with country clustering, predictive models, and data-driven recommendations for international organisations, policymakers, and NGOs allocating development aid.

Data Analysis & Visualisations

GDP vs Life Expectancy

Linear regression analysis showing the relationship between GDP per capita and life expectancy across all 200+ countries.

Every $1,000 GDP increase → +0.2 years life expectancy (y = 67.70 + 0.000208 × GDP)

Variable Correlations

Heatmap showing relationships between key development indicators — internet access, GDP, life expectancy, and education.

0.70 correlation between internet usage and life expectancy — digital access impacts health outcomes

Internet Usage Growth 2000–2025

Trend analysis of global internet adoption over 25 years, with a dramatic spike in growth around 2013 followed by steady maturation.

500% growth over the period — technology as a key driver of recent development progress

Country Clustering Analysis

Elbow method and silhouette scores determining the optimal number of development clusters among 200+ nations.

k=4 clusters with silhouette 0.46 — Advanced Digital, Emerging Growth, Transitional, Basic Needs

Analytical Insights

  • The regression model (y = 67.70 + 0.000208 × GDP) explains economic prosperity's correlation with longer life expectancy
  • Strong correlation (0.70) between internet usage and life expectancy highlights digital infrastructure as a development priority
  • Four distinct country clusters enable targeted policy interventions rather than one-size-fits-all approaches
  • GDP impact on life expectancy shows diminishing returns above $20,000 per capita — different priorities for different income levels
